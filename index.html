Carnivals For Children On Wheels

By the age of fourteen, I started the not-for-profit program, Carnivals For Children On Wheels which brought dozens of free carnivals to over 4000 youth who were underprivileged, mentally and physically disabled youth, and/or those with life threatening illnesses.

As the founder of the concept, I managed and created all the aspects of the program from organizing transportation for 100 volunteers, to building booths, expansion strategy, sourcing carnival prizes from various manufacturers, finding potential locations inside and outside for the events, organizing fundraising events, recruitment of corporate sponsorship, creation of marketing strategy, as well as speaking with and creating partnerships with directors of individual non-profit organizations.

The program was recognized as a successful venture among many in the non-profit sector. Our work has been recognized by many organizations and government agencies including the United States Presidential Service Award, The New Jersey General Assembly, as well as the USS Intrepid.

  • Position: Founder & Executive Director
  • Date: Aug 2007 - Aug 2012
  • Press: Jewish Standard, Intrepid Air & Space Museum

Startup Village

UStartup Village is a Community of Student & Alumni Entrepreneurs from the University of Maryland. Within 3 months of launching, the companies did a combined $250,000+ in revenue, created 20+ jobs, and fostered an amazing dog in partnership with the Prince George's County Humane Society.
